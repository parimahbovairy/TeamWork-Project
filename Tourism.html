The 50 most visited tourist attractions in the world

Niagara Falls, waterfall on the Niagara River in northeastern North America, one of the continent’s most
famous spectacles. The falls lie on the border between Ontario, Canada, and New York state, U.S. For many
decades the falls were an attraction for honeymooners and for such stunts as walking over the falls on
a tightrope or going over them in a barrel. Increasingly, however, the appeal of the site has become
its beauty and uniqueness as a physical phenomenon.

"The Statue of Liberty Enlightening the World" was a gift of friendship from the people of France to the
United States and is recognized as a universal symbol of freedom and democracy. The Statue of
Liberty was dedicated on October 28, 1886. It was designated as a National Monument in 1924.
Employees of the National Park Service have been caring for the colossal copper statue since 1933.
